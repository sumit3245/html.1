Hava Mahal Step into the royal charm of Hawa Mahal, one of Jaipur’s
most iconic and photogenic landmarks. Built in 1799 by
Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, this five-story palace is
made of red and pink sandstone, standing tall in the
heart of the city.
With 953 small windows (jharokhas), Hawa Mahal was designed
for royal women to observe daily life and festivals without
being seen. The cool breeze passing through these windows
gave it the name “Palace of Winds.”
From its honeycomb structure to the intricate latticework
and elevated views of the old city, Hawa Mahal is a stunning
example of Rajput and Mughal architecture.

– The Astronomical Wonder of Jaipur

Step into a world of science and history at Jantar Mantar,
Jaipur’s UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built in 1734 by the
scholarly king Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, this incredible
open-air observatory is a showcase of ancient astronomy.
It houses 19 giant instruments made of stone and marble,
designed to measure time, predict eclipses, track stars,
and observe planets — all with amazing accuracy, and without
modern technology.
The highlight is the Samrat Yantra, the world’s largest
stone sundial, which can tell the time with a precision
of just 2 seconds!
Whether you're a science lover or just curious, Jantar
Mantar offers a unique experience of how India’s ancient
astronomers read the skies.

Galta Gate

– Gateway to Spiritual Serenity

Galta Gate is the grand entrance to the sacred Galta
Ji Temple, also known as the Monkey Temple of Jaipur.
Located on the eastern edge of the city, Galta Gate marks
the beginning of a peaceful journey into the hills, where
ancient temples, natural springs, and holy kunds (water tanks) await.
Surrounded by Aravalli hills, the gate itself is built in
traditional Rajasthani style with a royal arch and stone
carvings, offering a majestic first impression before
entering the spiritual zone.
As you pass through Galta Gate, you step away from the
city's noise and into a sacred valley filled with devotion,
monkeys, panoramic views, and holy water believed to cleanse sins.

Jal Mahal

– Jal Mahal – A Palace on Water

GJal Mahal, meaning "Water Palace," is one of the most
mesmerizing architectural wonders in Jaipur, Rajasthan.
It sits quietly in the middle of Man Sagar Lake, appearing
to float on the serene waters. Surrounded by the majestic
Aravalli hills and a tranquil environment, this palace is
a symbol of Rajput elegance and engineering brilliance.
History and Architecture
Jal Mahal was built in the 18th century by Maharaja Madho
Singh I as a lodge for royal duck hunting trips. It was
never meant to be a palace for residence. Later, Maharaja
Jai Singh II made significant renovations, adding Mughal-style
gardens and features.
The palace is constructed in red sandstone and showcases a
beautiful blend of Rajput and Mughal architecture. It has
five floors, but during monsoon or when the lake is full,
four floors remain submerged underwater, giving it a magical
appearance.
