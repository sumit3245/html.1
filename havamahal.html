Hawa Mahal is one of the most iconic landmarks of Jaipur
and a symbol of Rajasthan’s royal heritage. Its name
literally means “Palace of Winds” or “Palace of Breeze”,
and it was designed to allow royal women to observe
street festivals while remaining unseen from the outside.

Unique Features

  • 953 small windows (called jharokhas) designed with intricate latticework.
  • The ventilation from these windows keeps the palace cool even in summer.
  • The structure resembles a honeycomb or the crown of Lord Krishna.
  • It is a five-storey building but has no stairs, only ramps.

Purpose

  • Built for the royal women (purdah system) to watch daily life and festivities on the street without being seen.
  • Also served as a summer retreat due to its excellent ventilation.

Architecture

  • A blend of Rajput architecture (domes, fluted pillars, floral patterns) and Mughal style (arches, stone inlay).
  • The front façade is like a screen or curtain, not a palace from the front.
  • From inside, the structure is more modest and simple.

Tips for Visitors

  • Best time to visit: Early morning (for sunlight and fewer crowds)
  • Don't miss the view of the city from the top floors.
  • Combine your visit with nearby attractions like City Palace, Jantar Mantar, and Bapu Bazaar.
