Overview of Galta Ji Temple:

  • Location: Galtaji, near Sisodia Rani Garden,
    Jaipur, Rajasthan.
  • Founded by: Saint Galav, a sage who performed
    penance here.
  • Primary Deity: Lord Hanuman, along with temples
    of Lord Ram, Krishna, Surya (Sun God), and Ganga.
  • Architectural Style: Traditional pink sandstone
    Rajasthani architecture.
  • Natural Water Spring: The temple complex is
    famous for its natural spring that fills a
    series of kunds (sacred water tanks).

History & Significance:

Galta Ji has been a holy site for centuries. According
to legend, the sage Rishi Galav lived and meditated
here for many years. Pleased with his devotion, the
gods caused a sacred stream to flow down from the
Gaumukh (cow‚Äôs mouth-shaped rock), forming natural
kunds.
The temple complex was later built in the 18th century
by Diwan Rao Kriparam, a courtier of Sawai Jai Singh
II, the ruler of Jaipur.

Monkeys at Galta Ji:

Galta Ji is home to hundreds of monkeys, especially
rhesus macaques. Visitors often feed them fruits and
nuts, and they are generally harmless, though playful
and active. The monkeys are considered sacred and
symbolic of Lord Hanuman.

Sacred Water Tanks (Kunds):

    There are seven kunds (ponds) in the complex.
    The most sacred are:
  • Galta Kund: Never dries up, considered the holiest.
  • Ram Kund
  • Sita Kund
    • Devotees bathe in these waters to cleanse themselves
    of sins, especially during holy festivals.

Important Events & Festivals:

    1. Makar Sankranti (14th January):
    • Biggest celebration at Galta Ji.
    • Thousands of pilgrims visit to bathe in
      the holy kunds.
    • Considered extremely auspiciou
    • Kite flying is also common across Jaipur
      on this day.
    2. Hanuman Jayanti:
    • Celebrated with special prayers and offerings
      to Lord Hanuman.
    • Devotees visit the temple early in the morning.
    3. Deepawali & Holi:
    • Special rituals and lighting of lamps are done.
    • The temple gets decorated and sees a large
      footfall.

Spiritual Importance:

  • Galta Ji is considered as holy as Haridwar or Varanasi by some local devotees.
  • People believe that bathing in the kunds washes away sins and grants spiritual peace.
  • Many sadhus and ascetics still meditate here in silence.

How to Reach:

  • By Road: 10 km from Jaipur city center.
  • Nearest Landmark: Sisodia Rani Garden. Sisodia Rani Garden.
  • Route: Narrow pathway leading through the Aravalli hills, walkable or via auto/rickshaw.
  • Trekking through the forested hills gives scenic views and monkey encounters.

Main Deities:

  • The main temple is dedicated to Lord Ram and Lord Hanuman.
  • Other temples are dedicated to Krishna, Surya (Sun God), and Balaji.
