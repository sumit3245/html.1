About Jaipur

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is one of India’s most culturally rich and visually stunning cities. Famously known as the Pink City, Jaipur got its nickname when the entire city was painted pink in 1876 to welcome the Prince of Wales. Today, it remains a symbol of royalty, heritage, and hospitality. Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, Jaipur is India's first planned city, designed according to ancient Indian Vastu Shastra. It is home to majestic forts, grand palaces, and bustling bazaars that reflect its glorious past. From the iconic Hawa Mahal and Amber Fort to the astronomical marvel Jantar Mantar and the elegant City Palace, every corner of Jaipur tells a story of royal legacy and architectural brilliance. Jaipur is also a shopper’s paradise. Its colorful markets like Johari Bazaar, Tripolia Bazaar, and Bapu Bazaar offer traditional Rajasthani jewelry, handicrafts, textiles, and blue pottery. The local cuisine — from Dal Baati Churma to Ghewar — adds delicious flavor to your visit. Whether you come for history, culture, food, or shopping, Jaipur offers an unforgettable experience. Surrounded by the Aravalli Hills and filled with vibrant festivals, warm people, and timeless charm, Jaipur is not just a destination — it's an emotion.

More About Jaipur

Jaipur, the capital city of Rajasthan, is one of India's most vibrant and culturally rich destinations. Known as the "Pink City" due to the distinct color of its buildings, Jaipur is a perfect blend of tradition and modernity. Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, the city was India’s first planned urban center, designed according to Vastu Shastra and Shilpa Shastra, the ancient Indian architectural sciences. Jaipur is famous for its majestic forts, royal palaces, colorful bazaars, and warm hospitality. Iconic landmarks like the Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds), City Palace, Jantar Mantar (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), and Amber Fort showcase the city’s regal past and architectural brilliance. Every corner of the city tells a story of valiant kings, artistic heritage, and royal lifestyle. Tourists from across the globe visit Jaipur not only for its historical marvels but also to experience its vibrant festivals like Teej, Gangaur, and the Jaipur Literature Festival. The city is also a paradise for shoppers, offering traditional handicrafts, colorful textiles, gemstones, and Rajasthani jewelry in bustling markets like Johari Bazaar, Bapu Bazaar, and Tripolia Bazaar. The cuisine of Jaipur is another major attraction. Rajasthani dishes such as Dal Baati Churma, Laal Maas, Ghevar, and Kachori offer a true taste of local flavor and spice. Despite its deep cultural roots, Jaipur has also embraced modernity with luxury hotels, educational institutions, and developing infrastructure, making it a rising hub for tourism and business. The city's connectivity via road, rail, and air is excellent, with Jaipur International Airport linking it to major cities in India and abroad. In 2019, Jaipur was declared a UNESCO World Heritage City, recognizing its historical and cultural significance. Whether you're an architecture enthusiast, a history lover, a foodie, or a curious traveler, Jaipur offers an unforgettable experience with its royal charm and colorful spirit.

About Govind dav ji

Govind Dev Ji Temple is a famous and sacred temple located in the City Palace complex of Jaipur, Rajasthan. It is dedicated to Lord Krishna, worshipped here as Govind Dev Ji. The idol of the deity was brought from Vrindavan by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, the founder of Jaipur, and installed in the temple in the 18th century. It is believed that this idol closely resembles Lord Krishna’s actual form. The temple holds great spiritual importance, especially among the devotees of the Gaudiya Vaishnav tradition. The idol was originally discovered by Sri Rupa Goswami, a disciple of Chaitanya Mahaprabhu. It is one of the seven main temples of Vrindavan-origin deities that were shifted to Jaipur to protect them during Mughal rule. Thousands of devotees visit the temple daily, especially during festivals like Janmashtami, Holi, and Radhashtami. The temple conducts seven daily aartis and bhog offerings, which are open to the public and follow a strict schedule. The peaceful atmosphere, devotional singing, and grand architecture make Govind Dev Ji Temple a must-visit religious site in Jaipur.

Jagat shiromani temple

Jagat Shiromani Temple is a beautiful and historic temple located in Amer (Amber), Jaipur, Rajasthan. It was built in the early 17th century by Queen Kanakwati, the wife of King Man Singh I, in memory of their son. The temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna, Lord Vishnu, and Meera Bai, the famous devotee of Krishna. One unique belief about this temple is that the idol of Lord Krishna here is the same idol worshipped by Meera Bai during her lifetime. The temple is known for its stunning Rajput and Mughal architecture, with intricately carved pillars, marble work, and grand archways. Located near the famous Amber Fort, the Jagat Shiromani Temple is a peaceful and sacred spot that attracts devotees and tourists alike. It stands as a symbol of devotion, art, and royal heritage in the city of Jaipur.


Papad ke Hanuman Ji Temple

Located near the historic city of Amer in Jaipur, Rajasthan, Papad ke Hanuman Ji Temple is a spiritually significant and culturally unique destination. Unlike traditional temples, the idol of Lord Hanuman here is not in a human-like form. Instead, it is a small, flat, and round-shaped stone that resembles a "papad" (a thin, crispy Indian snack), hence the name "Papad ke Hanuman Ji". This distinctive form is deeply respected and worshipped by thousands of devotees who believe in its miraculous powers. The temple is known for its peaceful and simple surroundings, often visited by those who are seeking relief from difficulties, negative energies, or any kind of obstacles in life. It is especially crowded on Tuesdays and Saturdays, which are considered highly auspicious for Lord Hanuman worship. Devotees offer flowers, oil, sweets, and sometimes even papads as a symbolic gesture of faith and gratitude. According to local beliefs, prayers made at this temple are quickly answered, and many people come here to perform rituals for protection, success, or healing. The spiritual atmosphere of the place is said to bring peace and strength to the devotees. The temple is also an important part of the cultural and religious fabric of Jaipur. While it may not be as architecturally grand as other temples in the region, Papad ke Hanuman Ji Temple holds a special place in the hearts of the local community. It is a perfect example of how faith and devotion are not always tied to appearances, but to belief and experience.


Old Govind Dev Ji Temple

A Divine Heritage of Jaipur The Old Govind Dev Ji Temple is one of the most revered and historically rich temples located in the city of Jaipur, Rajasthan. Dedicated to Lord Krishna, the temple is considered a sacred place of worship and a symbol of Jaipur's deep spiritual heritage. It is called “Old” to distinguish it from the newer Govind Dev Ji Temple inside the City Palace complex. This ancient shrine is believed to have existed before the city of Jaipur was officially established in the 18th century by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II. The idol of Govind Dev Ji (Lord Krishna) enshrined here is said to have been brought from Vrindavan, and is believed to closely resemble Krishna’s actual human form. According to legend, the idol was carved by Bajranabh, the great-grandson of Krishna, who had never seen Krishna in real life. He sculpted three idols based on descriptions from Krishna’s family, and Govind Dev Ji is said to be one of them. The temple's architecture reflects traditional Rajasthani style with ornate pillars, jharokhas (balconies), and intricate stone carvings. The surroundings have a peaceful, old-world charm that draws devotees seeking blessings and solace. Every day, thousands of devotees gather to witness aarti (prayer ceremonies), especially during Janmashtami and other Krishna-related festivals. Despite the presence of the newer temple in the City Palace, many locals still hold a deep emotional and spiritual connection with the Old Govind Dev Ji Temple. It is a place where tradition meets faith, where time slows down in the presence of divine love. The chants of "Radhe-Krishna" echo through the walls, creating a deeply spiritual atmosphere. For visitors interested in spirituality, history, or architecture, this temple offers a glimpse into Jaipur’s sacred past and the living devotion of its people.


Galta Ji Temple

The Sacred Monkey Temple of JaipurGalta Ji Temple, also known as the Monkey Temple, is one of the most ancient and sacred Hindu pilgrimage sites located about 10 km east of Jaipur, in the state of Rajasthan, India. Nestled in the Aravalli Hills, the temple complex is built around a natural mountain spring that forms several sacred kunds (water tanks) where pilgrims come to bathe and purify their souls. The temple is dedicated mainly to Saint Galav, a revered Hindu sage who is believed to have meditated here for many years. The temple complex houses shrines of Lord Ram, Lord Krishna, Lord Hanuman, and Surya (Sun God), but the Hanuman Temple within is especially famous because of the large population of monkeys that roam freely around the site. These monkeys, mostly of the rhesus macaque species, are both playful and sacred, and hence the temple is popularly called the Monkey Temple. The architecture of Galta Ji is stunning, featuring pink sandstone, traditional Rajput design, and intricate carvings. It resembles a grand haveli (mansion) more than a typical temple, and its setting between narrow rocky hills adds to its charm. From the hilltop, you also get a breathtaking panoramic view of Jaipur city. One of the main attractions is the natural spring, which flows into the temple’s sacred water tanks. Devotees believe that a dip in these holy waters can cleanse sins and bring blessings. The Makar Sankranti festival sees thousands of pilgrims visiting Galta Ji to bathe in these waters. Galta Ji is not only a religious site but also a cultural and natural treasure of Jaipur. It is a serene place that combines devotion, nature, and heritage, making it a must-visit for tourists, photographers, and spiritual seekers alike.
